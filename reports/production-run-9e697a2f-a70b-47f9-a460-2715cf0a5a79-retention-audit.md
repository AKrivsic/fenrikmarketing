# Retention Audit — Production Run `9e697a2f-a70b-47f9-a460-2715cf0a5a79`

_Read-only creative retention analysis. No implementation proposals. Judged as if published on TikTok today._

**Project:** fenrik Studio (`163c1822-ad30-4cee-8826-dfacd9c188b9`)  
**Run status:** completed  
**Packages reviewed:** 21 / 21  
**Completed video jobs in export:** 7 (packages 2, 6, 10, 11, 13, 17, 19); remaining 14 audited from package creative (voiceover + scene 1 prompts)  
**Observed analytics context:** ~2.25s average watch · 0–3% completion

---

## 1. Executive summary

These openings do not fail because the product is bad or the topics are wrong. They fail because **the first 2 seconds almost never create a reason to stay**.

Across all 21 packages, the system consistently opens with **calm explanation, soft observation, or story setup**, paired with **polished, metaphorical, low-event AI stills** (empty boards, desks, cafés, planning walls). Curiosity, surprise, tension, and visual novelty are rare. Several openings begin with brand/offer language and read as ads immediately.

In the ~2.25 seconds viewers currently give you, they typically hear an incomplete setup sentence and see a quiet generic workspace metaphor. That combination matches a 2-second drop-off almost perfectly.

**Bottom line:** retention is breaking at the scroll-stop layer, before story, before product, before payoff.

## 5. Biggest repeated problems

1. **Opens with explanation or context, not a scroll-stop.** “Everyone says…”, “Most businesses…”, “Most people hear…”, “Here’s what nobody tells you…” delay the point.
2. **First visuals are calm metaphors, not events.** Empty boards, desks, cafés, soft light, faceless figures. Low contrast against typical TikTok feeds.
3. **Curiosity / surprise / tension almost never appear in second 0–2.** The system prefers empathy essays over open loops.
4. **Spoken hook is weaker than the written hook.** The best line often never leads the VO.
5. **In 2.25 seconds, viewers hear incomplete setup.** Many first sentences are long; by drop-off the claim hasn’t landed.
6. **Product / offer sometimes opens the video** (P15, P20) → immediate ad recognition.
7. **Motion (when present) is low-intensity EXPLAIN / gentle zoom.** Does not create visual interruption.
8. **Aesthetic sameness:** polished AI still + restrained palette + “no readable text” → predictable branded content look.
9. **Payoff and product arrive late** (scene 3–4 or only as soft CTA), after the watch window has already closed.

---

## 6. Biggest strengths

1. **Topic relevance is real** — empty feeds, time cost, multi-platform tax, decision fatigue. Audience-true problems.
2. **Tone of voice is human**, not corporate jargon. Confessions and “I’ll be honest” land as authentic when they lead.
3. **A few spoken openings are structurally strong** when they lead with a punch:
   - P13: “The tasks that stay on your list the longest are never the hard ones.”
   - P18: “Most businesses don’t have a content problem.” (sets up contradiction)
   - P21: quantified weekly time loss
   - P8: agency cost number tease
4. **Some stored hooks are excellent** (questions, compressed contrasts) — the raw material exists, it just often isn’t what plays first.
5. **Visual metaphors are coherent with the message** (empty board = silent account). Conceptually clear; retention-weak, not meaning-weak.

## 7. Estimated reasons for ~2.25s average watch time

At ~2.25s the typical viewer has experienced:

| What they got | What they needed |
| --- | --- |
| Soft voiceover setup / incomplete sentence | A reason to stay in <1s |
| Quiet AI desk/board/café still | A visually interruptive first frame |
| Relatability without stakes | Curiosity, surprise, or tension |
| Possible ad smell (offer / polished brand film) | Native-feeling content impulse |

**Estimated contribution to early drop-off:**

| Factor | Est. contribution |
| --- | ---: |
| Opening visual fails to stop scroll | **35%** |
| Opening sentence is setup/explanation, not a hook | **28%** |
| No curiosity / surprise / tension in first 2s | **18%** |
| Looks predictable / polished / ad-like | **12%** |
| Payoff / conflict / product arrive too late (pacing) | **7%** |

This distribution explains both **ultra-low average watch** (leave before second 3) and **near-zero completion** (never enter the body of the video).

## 8. Prioritised list of problems (no fixes)

Ranked by expected impact on first-2-second retention:

1. **Opening visual has almost no scroll-stopping power** (calm metaphors, empty boards, desks, cafés).
2. **Spoken first line starts in explanation / observation mode** instead of a compulsion loop.
3. **System avoids surprise, tension, conflict, and true curiosity openers.**
4. **Best hooks often live in metadata, not in the first heard words.**
5. **First frames look like branded / AI / ad content** — polished, textless, predictable.
6. **First sentences are often too long to land inside the current watch window.**
7. **Product/offer sometimes opens the piece**, accelerating “this is an ad” exits.
8. **Pacing saves conflict and payoff for mid/late beats** the viewer never reaches.
9. **Motion does not interrupt** — when present, it explains rather than grabs.

## Part 6 — Pacing pattern (systemic)

Typical arc across the run:

| Beat | When it usually arrives | Inside 2.25s? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Curiosity loop | Rare; mild tease at best | Mostly no |
| Conflict | Soft (“empty feed”, “Friday plan dies”) — low stakes | Rarely felt |
| Surprise | Almost never | No |
| Product | Scene 3–4 when present; often absent/soft CTA | No |
| Payoff | End of VO | No |

**Yes — they spend too long building context.** The videos are written like short essays with a late turn. TikTok behaviour rewards an early spike; this inventory postpones the spike until after exit.

## Part 8 — Systemic patterns

**Why leave after ~2 seconds?**  
Nothing interruptive happens. Audio is mid-setup. Visual is a quiet metaphor. Brain classifies: “brand content / not for me / already know this vibe” → swipe.

**What repeats across most openings?**  
Emptiness metaphors · desk/table stills · soft relatability · explanation framing · polished AI aesthetic · “content about not posting content.”

**What does the system consistently avoid?**  
Questions as first spoken line · surprise · tension · conflict with stakes · ugly/raw/native texture · readable on-screen text spikes · chaotic or highly novel first frames · immediate pattern interrupts.

**What does the system consistently overuse?**  
“Here’s what nobody…” / “Most people/businesses…” setups · empty planning boards · calm desks · confession-as-essay · empathy without a hook spike · late product.
