# Marketing creative review — client lens

**Role:** Reviewing finished ads for a paying client.  
**Not reviewing:** pipeline, prompts, models, implementation.  
**Question:** Would these videos achieve a marketing objective in the wild?

**Sample:** 11 primary videos with enough creative evidence (stills, VO, audits). Judged as feed ads (TikTok / IG / LinkedIn short).

**Scoring keys used in averages**
- Scroll-stop: Q1 (1–10)
- Clarity: Yes=8 / Partial=5 / No=2 (mapped from Q2)
- Trust: Q6 (1–10)
- Product understanding: Q5 (1–10)
- Click intent: Would click=8 / maybe=5 / ignore=2 (mapped from Q8)
- Marketing score: mean of (scroll-stop, clarity-mapped, interest 1–10, problem 1–10, product, trust, click-mapped)

## Top 10 marketing weaknesses

1. Opens that don’t communicate the problem in 3 seconds (`36da`, `328a`).  
2. Product invisible or abstract until too late (most Fenrik).  
3. Pain explained in VO instead of shown (`36da`, `04911` mid, `328a`).  
4. Curiosity spike that isn’t paid off (`04911`).  
5. “Proof” frames that prove nothing (blank laptop).  
6. Soft CTAs with no urgency, proof, or offer shape.  
7. AI-still aesthetic → low brand trust.  
8. Cast/world discontinuity → feels assembled, not observed.  
9. Clever metaphors that win creatives and lose customers.  
10. Funnel mismatch: awareness labels on conversion-shaped cuts (or problem ads that never make the problem obvious).

---

## Top 10 marketing strengths

1. Real ICP pains when filmed as behavior (`4ab` phones, `d893` freeze, `684a` checkout).  
2. Scroll-stop hooks when odd or human (`04911` line, `d893` walk-in).  
3. Causal micro-stories (`7628340e` tab close, `684a` wrong card).  
4. Real product UI / end cards when present (`d893`, `684a`, `4ab` asset).  
5. Confession/openers that create self-ID (`a6e`).  
6. Clear commercial insight in VO even when visuals lag (Fenrik silent-website thesis).  
7. Emotion arcs that work when human faces lead (`4ab`, `d893`).  
8. Metaphor callback craft (`2fbd`) — brandable if ever readable.  
9. Explicit asks that convert intent (`d893` / `684a` CTA cards).  
10. Category ritual ads outperform abstract SaaS essays (`684a` vs Fenrik boards).

## Single biggest reason these videos would fail in the real market

**They often don’t earn the first three seconds — and when they do, they still don’t make the product’s job obvious before the viewer leaves.**

Feed ads live or die on instant problem recognition + a believable “this fixes that.” Too many cuts win neither: cryptic opens, essay middles, and product that never becomes concrete.

---

## Single biggest commercial improvement

**Make every ad a visible human problem in second 0–3, and a visible product job before the end — same person, same world, one clear ask.**

Not prettier AI. Not cleverer metaphors. **Stop-scroll situation → felt consequence → undeniable “what it does” → click reason.**

That is the gap between the best ads in this set (`d893`, `684a`, partly `4ab`) and the ones that would burn spend (`36da`, `328a`).
